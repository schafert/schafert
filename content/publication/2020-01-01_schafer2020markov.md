abstract = "The use of accelerometers in wildlife tracking provides a fine-scale               data source for understanding animal behavior and decision making.               We modeled transitions between flying, feeding, stationary and walking               behavior states using a first-order Bayesian Markov model, introducing               Polya-Gamma latent variables for automatic sampling of covariate               coefficients from the posterior distribution. Our model provides a               unifying framework for including both acceleration and GPS data, with               straightforward inference of behavioral time allocation across used               habitats."
