abstract = "Agent-based methods allow for defining simple rules that generate
              complex group behaviors. Instead of making simplifying assumptions
              across all anticipated scenarios, inverse reinforcement learning
              provides inference on the short-term (local) rules governing
              long-term behavior policies by using properties of a Markov decision
              process. We use the linearly-solvable Markov decision process to
              learn the local rules governing collective movement for a self-propelled
              particle simulation and a captive guppy population application."
